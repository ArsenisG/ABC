Academic Behavioural Confidence

Your responses to the set of 17 questions give a profile of your Academic Behavioural Confidence (ABC). Your profile reflects your beliefs about your academic-related behaviours and your sense of your ability to meet academic goals. Your profile is broken down into four dimensions, each of which support your academic performance:

  • Attendance (Att): Your Confidence in your willingness to appropriately attend your formal learning activities, such as lectures and tutorials.
  • Grades (Grd): Your confidence in completing the activities that contribute directly to your grades, including coursework and exams.
  • Studying (Std): Your confidence in undertaking the self-organised study necessary for your learning.
  • Verbalising (Vrb): Your confidence in your ability to verbalise your knowledge in your learning activities, such as lectures and tutorials.

What next?

Your results should prompt you to reflect on your academic confidence regarding attending and participating in learning events, as well as your personal study and preparation for assessments. Consider what, if anything, you wish to improve. You may wish to attend some of the relevant workshops on academic study skills provided by student support and wellbeing or discuss how to improve your academic confidence at your next personal tutor meeting.
